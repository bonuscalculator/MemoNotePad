✦ Tips & Tricks

Get More from Your Word Count Tool

Pro strategies for students, writers, and content professionals.

01

Paste First, Edit Second

Paste your full draft into the counter first to see its current word and character count. Then trim or expand as needed while watching the live stats change.

02

Use Keyword Density for SEO

Aim for a primary keyword density of 1–2% (about 15–30 mentions in a 1,500-word article). The top keywords panel makes this easy to check at a glance.

03

Check Speaking Time Before Presenting

Paste your speech script and check the speaking time estimate. If your 5-minute slot shows 8 minutes in the tool, trim before rehearsal — not on stage.

04

Meta Descriptions Should Be 120–160 Chars

Use the Meta Desc preset to ensure your meta description sits in the optimal range. Descriptions over 160 characters get cut off in Google search results.

05

Watch Unique Word Ratio

Divide unique words by total words for your vocabulary ratio. A ratio below 30% often indicates repetitive writing. Aim for 40–60% in polished prose.

06

Download a Copy of Every Draft

Always download your text with the toolbar button. It's a one-click backup that keeps your work safe locally — no cloud, no login, no risk.
